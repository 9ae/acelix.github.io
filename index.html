The Pencil to Code Philosophy

In my career as a developer, I spent 3 years working closely with designers as a frontend engineer. A majority of it was spent measuring pixels, trapped in a feedback loop between designer and developer, both trying very hard to make sure our visions align. I empathized with the designer, who wants their layout mockup implemented as they invisioned it, but lacks the skillset to do it themselves. I also longed for the days, when I got to exercise my creative visions. As a frontend developer, I was essentially working as a translator. The truth is most work produced by both parties are redundant. As someone who taught herself Photoshop and HTML/CSS, I know that one is not anymore difficult or time consuming than the other. Design tools are very great for generating graphical assets, but are not optimized for iterative design. Work becomes redundant when you have to make global changes, or make modifications to the visuals that are state dependent. Laying out your design in code, may involve more overhead at first, but your process becomes exponentially faster as your app/site screens grow.

Pencil to Code is a fluid design approach to produce prototypes quickly. Its mission is to make work creative and invigorating for both designers and developers.
